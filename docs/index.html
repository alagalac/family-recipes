Spag Bol

One of our quick and easy go to meals. The carrots are the secret to making the sauce sweet and tasty.
Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Servings: 4 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can tomato puree (400g)
  • 500g spaghetti
  • 300g minced beef

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add the onion, and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the minced beef and cook until browned.
  3. Add the grated carrots and cook for a few more minutes.
  4. Stir in the tomato puree and simmer for 10 minutes. Add salt to season.
  5. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti according to package instructions.
  6. Serve the sauce over the spaghetti.

Notes

  • Omit the minced beef for a vegetarian version.
  • Serve with grated cheese on top.
  • You can also use chopped or crushed tomatoes instead of tomato puree.

Chicken & Leek Pasties

The pastry from this recipe is amazing BTW. I used it to make beef wellingtons for some friends once and they were dreaming about the pastry months later. If there's anything you take from this recipe, it's the pastry!
Prep Time: 1 hour | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Servings: 4 (4 pasties)

Ingredients

Rough puff pastry

  • 300g plain flour
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • 150g chilled unsalted butter, cut into small cubes

Filling

  • 25g butter
  • 2-3 medium leeks (about 500g), trimmed and finely sliced
  • 1 teaspoon roughly chopped thyme leaves
  • 150ml double cream
  • 1 teaspoon English mustard
  • About 400g boned chicken thigh and breast meat (or leftover cooked chicken), cut into thick slices
  • 1 tablespoon rapeseed or olive oil (if using fresh chicken)
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

To finish

  • 1 medium egg, lightly beaten with 1 teaspoon milk, for glazing

Instructions

  1. To make the pastry, mix the flour with the salt, then add the cubed butter and toss until the pieces are coated with flour. Add just enough iced water (8-10 tablespoons) to bring the mixture together into a fairly firm dough.
  2. Shape the dough into a rectangle with your hands and, on a well-floured surface, roll it out in one direction, away from you, so you end up with a rectangle about 1cm thick. Fold the far third towards you, then fold the nearest third over that (rather like folding a business letter), so that you now have a rectangle made up of 3 equal layers. Give the pastry a quarter-turn, then repeat the rolling, folding and turning process 5 more times. Wrap the pastry in a wax wrap or baking paper and rest it in the fridge for about 30 minutes, or up to an hour.
  3. To make the filling, melt the butter in a frying pan, add the leeks and thyme, and sweat gently for 5-10 minutes, until the leeks are very tender. Stir in the cream and cook gently for 4-5 minutes to reduce and thicken. Stir in the mustard, season well and leave to cool.
  4. If using fresh chicken, season well and fry in the oil in a frying pan over a medium-high heat until evenly browned and cooked through, then leave to cool.
  5. Roll out the pastry on a lightly floured work surface to about 3mm thick. Using a plate or a cake tin as a template, cut out four 20cm circles; you may have to gather up the trimmings, lay them on top of each other, and re-roll them to get your fourth circle.
  6. Spoon the leek mixture on to one half of each circle and pile the chicken on top. Brush the pastry edges with a little water, fold the other half of the pastry over the filling to form a half-moon shape and crimp well to seal.
  7. Place on a lightly oiled baking sheet and brush the tops of the pasties with beaten egg. Bake in an oven preheated to 190°C/Gas Mark 5 for about 25 minutes, until the pastry is golden brown. Eat hot or cold.

Mini Meatloaves with Carrot Potato Mash

The girls really love these mini meatloaves, and they don't usually like meat otherwise.
Prep Time: 25 minutes | Cook Time: 20-30 minutes | Servings: 5

Ingredients

  • Meatloaves:
  • 1 brown onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 parsnip
  • 450g beef and pork mince
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 Tbsp gluten-free soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 Tbsp chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup grated cheese
  • Mash:
  • 600g potatoes
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 Tbsp finely chopped chives
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Grease a 12-hole muffin tin (or loaf tin measuring about 25cm x 16cm). Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil. Bring a full kettle to the boil. Finely dice onion; grate carrot; peel and grate parsnip.
  2. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large fry-pan on medium heat. Fry onion, carrot and parsnip about 4 minutes, until tender.
  3. In a large bowl, combine mince, egg, breadcrumbs, soy sauce, salt, parsley, half the tomato sauce and half the cheese. Season with pepper.
  4. Add cooked vegetables to the bowl with mince mixture. Mix well, using clean hands. Press about 1/4 cup of mixture into the muffin tins. Pack mixture into muffin or loaf tin tightly, so it stays together once baked. Top with remaining tomato sauce and cheese. Bake for 20 minutes (muffin tins) or 30 minutes (loaf tin).
  5. While meatloaves cook, cut potatoes and carrot into 2cm pieces. Cook in pot of boiling water, for about 12-15 minutes, until very soft. Mash with butter, milk, chives and salt, until smooth. Cover, to keep warm.
  6. Set cooked meatloaves aside, to rest for 5 minutes.

Notes

  • Serve with a side of steamed vegetables such as broccoli for a complete meal.
  • Alternatively just do normal mash potatoes with a bit of wholegrain mustard.

Pita Nachos

We find a lot of the corn chips are too salty, so we prefer to make our own pita chips. They get super crispy.
Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 packet pita bread, each cut into 8 triangles
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 500g beef mince, or brown lentils for vegetarian
  • 1 tin tomatoes (400g)
  • 1 tin black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tbsp taco seasoning (or mix 1 tsp each ground cumin, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, dried oregano, and a pinch of cayenne pepper)
  • 1 avocado
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1/4 cup grated cheese

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (fan 180°C). Line a baking tray with baking paper.
  2. Prepare the pita chips: lightly brush or spray pita triangles with oil, spread in a single layer on the tray and bake 8-12 minutes until crisp and golden. Remove and set aside.
  3. Meanwhile heat a splash of oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook, stirring, for 2-3 minutes until softened and translucent.
  4. Add the beef mince (or cooked brown lentils). Season with salt and pepper and cook, breaking up with a spoon, until browned and cooked through (about 6-8 minutes).
  5. Stir in the tin of tomatoes, beans, and taco seasoning (or spice mix). Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat and cook gently for 10-15 minutes until the sauce has reduced and thickened. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  6. If using avocado and fresh tomato as toppings, prepare them now: dice tomato and mash the avocado; squeeze a little lemon or lime over the avocado to prevent browning.
  7. To assemble: scatter the pita chips on a serving platter or divide between plates, spoon the mince mixture over the chips, then top with grated cheese, diced tomato, avocado and other toppings such as olives, sour cream or salsa.

Notes

  • You can use regur corn chips (we prefer the mexicano natural ones), but we like the pita chips better.

Chicken Chroizo Paella

One of our top recipes that we cooked when living in the UK. We loved using good quality spanish chorizo.
Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 red peppers
  • 1 yellow pepper
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 1 small bunch flat leaf parsley
  • 1 punnet cherry tomatoes
  • 5 chicken thighs (boneless, skinless)
  • 1 chicken stock pot
  • 4 sprigs rosemary
  • 2 diced chorizo sausages (about 150g)
  • 350g basmati rice
  • 1 lemon
  • 600ml boiling water
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Remove the stalks and cores from the peppers and cut into thin slices. Peel and grate the garlic, finely chop the parsley leaves, and quarter the cherry tomatoes. Chop the chicken thighs into bite-sized chunks. Dissolve the chicken stock pot in 600ml boiling water. Pull the rosemary leaves from their sprigs.
  2. Heat a splash of olive oil in a large frying pan on high heat. Season the chicken with salt and pepper. Add the chicken to the pan and fry for a few minutes until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add another splash of olive oil to the pan. Add the peppers and tomatoes. Cook for a few minutes until softened. Add the garlic, rosemary leaves, and chorizo. Cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Add the rice and half of the parsley. Cook for a few minutes until the rice absorbs the oil.
  5. Add the chicken stock and browned chicken. Stir well. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook for 15-20 minutes. Do not stir while cooking to allow a crust to form on the bottom.
  6. Once the liquid is absorbed, remove the pan from the heat. Cover with a clean tea towel and let rest for 5 minutes. Serve garnished with the remaining parsley and lemon wedges.

Chicken Katsu Curry

They used to have a Wagamama in Wellington, and I loved going there for the katsu curry. It turns out they still have them in the UK, and so we often went there for katsu curry too!
Prep Time: 10 mins | Cook Time: 15 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

For the dish

  • 120g rice (any rice will do!)
  • 2 skinless chicken breasts
  • 50g plain flour
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 100g panko breadcrumbs
  • 75ml vegetable oil, for deep-frying
  • 40g mixed salad leaves

For the sauce (from scratch)

  • 2-3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, crushed
  • 2.5cm piece of ginger, peeled and grated
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 heaped tbsp mild curry powder
  • 1 tbsp plain flour
  • 300ml chicken or vegetable stock
  • 100ml coconut milk
  • 1 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar, to taste

Instructions

Make the sauce

  1. Heat 2-3 tbsp vegetable oil in a saucepan. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger and cook until softened.
  2. Add the curry powder and turmeric, and cook for another minute on low to medium heat.
  3. Stir in 1 tbsp flour and cook for 1 minute with the spices.
  4. Gradually add the chicken or vegetable stock, then the coconut milk. Add a pinch of sugar, salt, and a dash of soy sauce. Simmer until thickened.
  5. Strain the sauce through a sieve, a little at a time, to make it smooth. Set aside and keep warm.

Make the dish

  1. Cook the rice according to the packet instructions.
  2. Slice each chicken breast in half horizontally to make 4 fillets. Coat each fillet in flour, dip in beaten egg, then coat in panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Heat the oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the chicken fillets until golden brown and cooked through. Drain on paper towels.
  4. Slice the chicken into strips. Serve with rice, salad leaves, and plenty of curry sauce.

Notes

For extra greens, serve with wok-fried vegetables or steamed broccoli.

Spaghetti Meatballs

Another easy spaghetti recipe.
Prep Time: 20 mins | Cook Time: 20 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 400g beef mince
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 50g breadcrumbs
  • 1 tsp dried mixed herbs
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 400g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 400g spaghetti
  • 20g grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. Place the beef mince, grated onion, egg, breadcrumbs, dried mixed herbs, and salt in a large bowl. Mix well until all ingredients are combined.
  2. Shape the mixture into small meatballs, about the size of a golf ball. I use a tablespoon scoop to measure and shape them evenly.
  3. Heat a large frying pan over medium heat and add a little oil. Cook the meatballs in batches, turning occasionally, until browned on all sides and cooked through (about 10-12 minutes). Remove the meatballs from the pan and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, add the minced garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the canned crushed tomatoes and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for about 10 minutes to thicken slightly. Add salt to season.
  5. While the sauce is simmering, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside, saving a little pasta water.
  6. Return the meatballs to the sauce and simmer for another 5 minutes to let the flavors meld together, adding a splash of pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.
  7. Serve the meatballs and sauce over the cooked spaghetti. Sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese before serving.

Notes

  • Pork mince (or a pork-beef mix) also works well in this recipe.
  • For extra vegetables, add a grated carrot or courgette to the sauce.
  • If available, fresh herbs work well in the meatball mixture instead of dried.
  • Add BBQ sauce (or even better Feijoa BBQ sauce) to the meatball mix for more flavour.

Spaghetti Carbonara (Traditional)

Kimmie likes creamy pasta dishes. I really like this more traditional version of carbonara, which is creamy without using cream.
Prep Time: 5m | Cook Time: 15m | Servings: 4

Ingredients

Main

  • 175g guanciale (or pancetta or block bacon), skin removed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 100g parmigiano reggiano, finely shredded (or pecorino romano, or parmesan)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 400g spaghetti
  • 1 tbsp cooking/kosher salt (for pasta water)
  • 1/2 cup pasta cooking water
  • 1 garlic clove, finely minced (optional)

Garnish (optional)

  • Parsley, finely chopped
  • Parmigiano reggiano

Instructions

  1. Cut guanciale into 0.5cm thick slices, then into batons.
  2. Place eggs and yolks in a large bowl. Whisk to combine, then stir in parmesan and pepper.
  3. Bring 4 litres of water to the boil with the salt. Add pasta and cook per packet directions.
  4. Just before draining, scoop out 1 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain pasta.
  5. While pasta is cooking, place guanciale in a non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Cook for 4-5 minutes until golden. No oil needed. If using garlic, add in the last minute.
  6. Tip hot pasta into the pan and toss to coat in guanciale fat.
  7. Transfer pasta and any residual fat in the pan into the bowl with the egg. Add 1/2 cup pasta cooking water. Stir vigorously for 1 minute until sauce transforms from watery to creamy and clings to pasta.
  8. Transfer into warm bowls. Serve immediately, garnished with extra parmigiano reggiano, black pepper, and parsley if desired.

Notes

  • Use bacon instead of guanciale and parmesan instead of parmigiano reggiano for a more economical version.

Baked Mac and Cheese

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | Servings:

Ingredients

Macaroni

  • 250g / 8 oz macaroni (elbow pasta)
  • 1 tbsp (15g) unsalted butter (or 2 tsp oil)

Topping

  • 2/3 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp (30g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Sauce

  • 4 tbsp (60g) unsalted butter
  • 1/3 cup plain / all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups milk, warmed (low or full fat)
  • 2 cups freshly shredded cheese (Gruyere recommended)
  • 1 cup freshly shredded mozzarella (or more cheese of choice)
  • 3/4 tsp salt

Seasonings (optional)

  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp mustard powder

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C.
  2. Cook pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to the boil. Add macaroni and cook per packet directions, but reduce cooking time by 1 minute (cook 'al dente'). Drain well.
  3. Toss in butter: Return drained pasta to the pot, add 1 tbsp butter and toss until melted and coating each piece. Set aside while you make the sauce (this helps stop the pasta swelling and becoming stodgy).
  4. Make the topping: Combine panko breadcrumbs, melted butter and 1/4 tsp salt in a small bowl. Set aside.
  5. Make the sauce (roux method): In a large saucepan or ovenproof skillet, melt 4 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add flour and cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute to make a roux.
  6. Add milk: Add about 1 cup of warmed milk, whisk to dissolve the roux, then add remaining milk and whisk until smooth and lump-free.
  7. Season and thicken: Add salt and any optional seasonings. Cook, stirring frequently, for 5-8 minutes until the sauce thickens to a cream consistency (it should coat the back of a spoon).
  8. Add cheese: Remove the sauce from the heat and stir in the shredded cheeses - they don't need to fully melt on the stove. Taste and adjust salt.
  9. Assemble: Pour the cheese sauce into the pot with the butter-coated macaroni and stir quickly to combine. Transfer the mixture to a baking dish or keep in an ovenproof skillet. Sprinkle the breadcrumb topping evenly over the surface.
  10. Bake: Bake at 180°C for about 25 minutes, or until the top is light golden and the sauce is bubbly. Do not overbake or the dish will dry out.
  11. Serve: Remove from oven and serve immediately, garnished with chopped parsley if desired.

Notes

  • Grate your own cheese for best results — pre-grated cheese often contains anti-caking agents that can make sauces grainy.
  • Tossing the cooked pasta with butter before adding sauce prevents the pasta from swelling and keeps the final dish saucy rather than stodgy.
  • This recipe reheats well; reheat gently and consider adding a splash of milk if the sauce has thickened in the fridge.
  • Make-ahead: Assemble without the breadcrumb topping, refrigerate, add breadcrumbs just before baking and bake until hot and golden.

Pineapple Fried Rice

This fried rice was a hit with the girls, especially the pineapple!
Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Servings: 4

Ingredients

Fried rice

  • 200g shelled prawns (defrosted), or thinly sliced chicken, or cubed tofu, or 3-4 eggs
  • 2-3 tablespoons oil (canola, peanut, sunflower or rice bran)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 3 cups finely sliced cabbage
  • 1 cup defrosted frozen peas or corn (or sliced green beans)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves or 1 small lime/lemon tree leaf, very finely sliced (optional)
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, finely chopped (optional)
  • 4-5 cups leftover cooked rice
  • 1 x 400g can pineapple chunks, well drained (reserve juice for other use)
  • 1/2 cup roasted chopped cashew nuts or peanuts

Fried rice sauce

  • 1 1/2 - 2 tablespoons sweet chilli sauce
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce or soy sauce (or 1 tablespoon each)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • Pinch of chilli flakes (optional)

Optional extras

  • Crispy fried shallots
  • Finely sliced spring onion (green part)
  • Chopped fresh chilli

Instructions

  1. Pat prawns/chicken/tofu dry and season with salt. Heat a drizzle of oil in a wok or large frypan over medium-high heat. Stir-fry protein for 4-5 minutes until cooked and golden. If using eggs, whisk with a pinch of salt and cook as an omelette, then break into pieces. Transfer protein/eggs to a large bowl and set aside.
  2. Add another 2 tablespoons oil to the wok and stir-fry onion, carrot, garlic, cabbage and peas/corn/green beans for about 5 minutes until starting to caramelise. Transfer vegetables to the bowl with the cooked protein.
  3. Mix all fried rice sauce ingredients together in a small bowl.
  4. Add a drizzle more oil to the pan, add turmeric, ground coriander, curry powder, kaffir lime leaf and lemongrass (if using) and sizzle for 30 seconds.
  5. Add cooked rice, drained pineapple, chopped cashews/peanuts and the fried rice sauce. Stir-fry and toss for about 5 minutes until well combined and heated through, allowing some rice to brown and form little crusty bits if desired.
  6. Return the cooked vegetables and protein/eggs to the pan, toss to combine, and season to taste with extra soy/fish sauce or salt.
  7. Serve topped with crispy shallots, spring onion and chopped chilli if using.

Palak Paneer Curry

This isn't a traditional palak paneer recipe, but it's what the kids like! The grated carrots help to bulk it out and sweeten the sauce.
Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 400g paneer, 1.5cm cubes
  • 150g baby spinach leaves
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 tin (400g) chopped tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tin (400ml) coconut cream (or use 1 cup cream)
  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cups basmati rice
  • Naan bread, to serve

Instructions

  1. Cook the basmati rice according to the package instructions.
  2. Cut the paneer into 1.5cm cubes.
  3. Heat oil in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the paneer cubes and cook, turning occasionally, until golden brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, add more oil if needed, then cook the diced onion and grated carrots until onion is softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  6. Stir in the garam masala and turmeric, and cook for 1 minute.
  7. Add the chopped tomatoes and coconut cream. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Cook for 10 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  8. Add the baby spinach leaves and cook until wilted, about 2-3 minutes.
  9. Blend with an immersion blender until smooth (optional). Season with salt to taste.
  10. Return the paneer to the pan and simmer for another 5 minutes to heat through.
  11. Serve the palak paneer curry over the cooked basmati rice with naan bread on the side.

Notes

  • For a non-vegetarian option you could use chicken thigh instead of paneer.

Corn Fritters

Sausage Rolls

Everybody likes sausage rolls! These ones work well cold too.
Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 1 brown onion, finely diced
  • 1 apple, grated
  • 1/2 carrot, grated
  • 500g sausage mince (or regular pork mince)
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 3 sheets puff pastry (thawed if frozen)
  • 2 tbsp milk

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper. Heat a drizzle of oil in a fry-pan on medium heat. Fry onion, carrot and apple for 3-4 minutes, until softened. Set aside to cool slightly.
  2. While vegetables are cooking, in a large bowl combine sausage mince, breadcrumbs, egg, and salt. Add the cooked vegetables and use a wooden spoon to mix well.
  3. Place pastry sheets on a flat surface, and cut in half with a sharp knife so you have 2 rectangles with short sides facing you (6 in total).
  4. Divide sausage mixture into 6 even portions and mould a portion into a log shape on the bottom third of each pastry piece. Roll pastry and sausage mixture away from you to enclose tightly, with about 2cm overlap of pastry for the seam. Trim off any excess pastry and reserve. Place roll on prepared tray, seam-side down.
  5. Brush sausage rolls with milk and bake for 25-30 minutes, until pastry is puffed and golden. Leave to cool on tray for at least 5 minutes before serving.

Notes

  • Use any leftover pastry to make small scrolls. Marmite and cheese works well.

Prep Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour 15 minutes | Servings: 4

Ingredients

Gnocchi

  • 700-800g agria potatoes, scrubbed (leave the skin on the potatoes)
  • 1 free-range egg
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • 150g '00' Italian flour (or plain flour)

Tomato Basil Sauce

  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 400g can Italian crushed tomatoes
  • 1 generous handful basil leaves
  • 150g fresh cow's mozzarella (or shredded/grated mozzarella)
  • 1 small handful parmesan, grated

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Place whole potatoes on an oven tray and bake for about 1 hour (depending on the size of potatoes) until very tender - test with a skewer or tip of a sharp knife; it should poke right through with no resistance.
  2. Cut potatoes in half and peel off the skins while still warm. Push warm potato flesh through a potato ricer or a sieve (with the help of the back of a large spoon) into a large bowl. This ensures light gnocchi; whatever you do, do not mash the potato.
  3. Add egg and salt and mix in with a fork. Sift in flour and use your hands to bring together as a soft dough. Add a little more flour if needed - it should feel dry to the touch, but not crumbly. Knead a few times, then divide into six pieces.
  4. On a lightly floured surface, roll each piece out into a log about 30cm long, then cut into bite-sized 2cm pieces.   6. Drop about half of the gnocchi (one by one) into the boiling water and cook for a couple of minutes, scooping them out with a slotted spoon as they bob to the surface. Transfer straight into the pan of hot tomato sauce.
  7. Once all the gnocchi are in the pan, gently toss to coat in sauce and top with mozzarella and parmesan. Place under the oven grill for about 5 minutes or until sauce is bubbling and mozzarella is melted. Serve immediately.

  • This recipe also works well with pumpkin or kumara instead of potato.

Hiroshima Okonomiyaki

We encountered Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki during our holiday to Japan. We love it and have included it here, but we don't make it that often as it is quite tricky and messy!
Prep Time: 5 mins | Cook Time: 20 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 40g flour
  • 80ml water
  • 1 tbsp bonito flakes
  • 5g tororo konbu (shaved dried konbu, optional, see notes)
  • 120g cabbage, shredded
  • 80g bean sprouts
  • 2 tbsp tenkasu (fried batter bits, see notes)
  • 1 tbsp shallots/scallions, chopped finely
  • 50g thinly sliced pork belly
  • 100-120g yellow noodles (cooked, see notes)
  • Oil
  • Salt & pepper
  • Topping: Aonori, Okonomiyaki sauce (see notes)

Instructions

  1. Heat a flat griddle pan or frypan to 170°C.
  2. Mix flour and water in a bowl until lump free.
  3. Oil the pan thinly and pour about ¾ of the batter onto the pan, spreading to a 20cm circle.
  4. Turn heat up to 200°C. Spread bonito flakes and tororo konbu (if using) on the batter.
  5. Pile cabbage and bean sprouts within the circle, about 8cm tall. Sprinkle a pinch of salt.
  6. Press down vegetables slightly, add tenkasu and shallots/scallions, then spread pork slices over. Sprinkle salt & pepper.
  7. Cook ~5 mins until edge of batter curves up. Do not move until this point.
  8. Spread remaining batter over the pile to glue toppings together.
  9. Slide spatula under, gently lift and turn over. Collect scattered vegetables and tidy up.
  10. Cook 8-10 mins until cabbage is wilted and soft.
  11. After 6 mins, stir fry noodles on unused area of pan or new frypan at 200°C for a couple of minutes. Shape noodles into a circle matching okonomiyaki diameter.
  12. Lift okonomiyaki and place on top of noodles.
  13. Reduce pan to 170°C. Clean and oil pan.
  14. Crack an egg onto oiled area, break yolk and spread to a circle matching okonomiyaki.
  15. While egg is half raw, lift okonomiyaki and place on egg.
  16. After 10-20 sec, lift and turn over so egg is on top.
  17. Spread okonomiyaki sauce over egg, sprinkle aonori.
  18. Cut in half and serve hot off the pan or transfer to plates.

Notes

  • Tororo konbu is optional; substitute with shio-konbu or increase bonito flakes to 2 tbsp if unavailable.
  • Tenkasu are fried bits from tempura; can be bought or collected when making tempura.
  • Use ready-to-use, boiled fresh, or boiled dried noodles. Cook to al dente or slightly harder.
  • Okonomiyaki sauce: Mix 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 3 tbsp tomato ketchup, 1 tsp soy sauce, 1 tsp honey, 1/2 tsp dashi stock powder diluted in 1 tsp water (optional).
  • Use the back of a ladle to spread batter in a circle.
  • Cover toppings with pork slices to prevent scattering when flipping.
  • Use two spatulas if needed for flipping.
  • If noodles are sticky, add some water.

Chicken & Leek Pasties

The pastry from this recipe is amazing BTW. I used it to make beef wellingtons for some friends once and they were dreaming about the pastry months later. If there's anything you take from this recipe, it's the pastry!
Prep Time: 1 hour | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Servings: 4 (4 pasties)

Ingredients

Rough puff pastry

  • 300g plain flour
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • 150g chilled unsalted butter, cut into small cubes

Filling

  • 25g butter
  • 2-3 medium leeks (about 500g), trimmed and finely sliced
  • 1 teaspoon roughly chopped thyme leaves
  • 150ml double cream
  • 1 teaspoon English mustard
  • About 400g boned chicken thigh and breast meat (or leftover cooked chicken), cut into thick slices
  • 1 tablespoon rapeseed or olive oil (if using fresh chicken)
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

To finish

  • 1 medium egg, lightly beaten with 1 teaspoon milk, for glazing

Instructions

  1. To make the pastry, mix the flour with the salt, then add the cubed butter and toss until the pieces are coated with flour. Add just enough iced water (8-10 tablespoons) to bring the mixture together into a fairly firm dough.
  2. Shape the dough into a rectangle with your hands and, on a well-floured surface, roll it out in one direction, away from you, so you end up with a rectangle about 1cm thick. Fold the far third towards you, then fold the nearest third over that (rather like folding a business letter), so that you now have a rectangle made up of 3 equal layers. Give the pastry a quarter-turn, then repeat the rolling, folding and turning process 5 more times. Wrap the pastry in a wax wrap or baking paper and rest it in the fridge for about 30 minutes, or up to an hour.
  3. To make the filling, melt the butter in a frying pan, add the leeks and thyme, and sweat gently for 5-10 minutes, until the leeks are very tender. Stir in the cream and cook gently for 4-5 minutes to reduce and thicken. Stir in the mustard, season well and leave to cool.
  4. If using fresh chicken, season well and fry in the oil in a frying pan over a medium-high heat until evenly browned and cooked through, then leave to cool.
  5. Roll out the pastry on a lightly floured work surface to about 3mm thick. Using a plate or a cake tin as a template, cut out four 20cm circles; you may have to gather up the trimmings, lay them on top of each other, and re-roll them to get your fourth circle.
  6. Spoon the leek mixture on to one half of each circle and pile the chicken on top. Brush the pastry edges with a little water, fold the other half of the pastry over the filling to form a half-moon shape and crimp well to seal.
  7. Place on a lightly oiled baking sheet and brush the tops of the pasties with beaten egg. Bake in an oven preheated to 190°C/Gas Mark 5 for about 25 minutes, until the pastry is golden brown. Eat hot or cold.

Focaccia Bread

This is my go-to focaccia recipe. A couple of times as potluck lunches other people have made focaccia bread too, but this one is always the best.
Prep Time: 1 hour 30 minutes | Cook Time: 15-20 minutes | Servings: 1 loaf

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely minced
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme or 1 teaspoon dried
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary or 1 teaspoon dried
  • 1/4 teaspoon fresh ground black pepper
  • 1 cup (235 ml) warm water
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1/4 teaspoon honey
  • 2 1/2 cups (325 grams) all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt

Instructions

  1. In a cold medium skillet, combine olive oil, minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, and the black pepper. Place the pan over low heat and cook, stirring occasionally, 5 to 10 minutes or until aromatic, but before the garlic browns. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the warm water, yeast, and honey. Stir a few times then let sit for 5 minutes. Add 1 cup of the flour and a 1/4 cup of the infused garlic-olive oil mixture. Stir 3 to 4 times until the flour has moistened. Let sit for another 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in the remaining 1 1/2 cups of flour and the salt. Once the dough comes together, transfer to a floured board and knead the dough 10 to 15 times until smooth. Transfer to a large oiled bowl, cover with a warm, damp towel and let rise for 1 hour. (It’s best to let the dough rise in a warmer area of your kitchen).

  5. Transfer the dough to the baking sheet then press it down into the pan. Use your fingers to dimple the dough then drizzle the top with the remaining 2 tablespoons of the garlic-olive oil mixture. Let the dough rise for 20 minutes until it puffs slightly.
  6. Bake until golden brown, 15 to 20 minutes. Cool baked focaccia bread on a wire rack.

South African Milk Tart

One of Jase's favourite desserts
Prep Time: 20 mins (plus cooling) | Cook Time: 30 mins | Servings: 8-10

Ingredients

smear_crust

  • 100g salted butter, softened
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 100g plain flour
  • 2 eggs (Size 7)

filling

  • 600ml milk (for the pot)
  • 150ml milk (for the mix)
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • 50g plain flour
  • 50g cornflour (cornstarch)
  • 2g table salt
  • 100g salted butter
  • 4 eggs, separated (Size 7)
  • 10ml vanilla essence
  • Ground cinnamon, to sprinkle

Instructions

  1. Prepare a 25cm pie dish with grease spray.
  2. For the smear crust: Cream 100g softened salted butter and 100g granulated sugar until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes.
  3. Add 2 eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition. Fold in 100g plain flour until the mixture is well combined.
  4. Using your fingers, smear a very thin, even layer of the mixture over the base and sides of the prepared pie dish. Use a light touch so the mixture distributes evenly; this forms a delicate thin crust. If you have extra mixture, freeze for later use.
  5. For the filling: Preheat the oven to 160°C (fan or conventional equivalent). Put 600ml milk into a pot and warm over medium-low heat until scalding (just below boiling).
  6. In a separate bowl, whisk together 150ml milk, 150g granulated sugar, 50g plain flour, 50g cornflour and 2g salt into a smooth, lump-free paste.
  7. While stirring, gradually add the hot scalded milk to the paste to temper it, then return the combined mixture to the pot. Heat over medium-low, stirring continuously, until the mixture reaches its first boil and forms a thick plop (bubbles).
  8. Transfer the hot milk mixture to a mixing bowl. Lightly whisk the 4 egg yolks and whisk them into the warm milk mixture. Add 100g salted butter and 10ml vanilla essence and stir until combined; the butter will melt into the warm custard. Set aside to cool slightly.
  9. In a clean bowl, whip the 4 egg whites to stiff peaks (but not dry).
  10. Once the milk mixture has cooled to warm (not hot), fold the whipped egg whites gently into the milk mixture until just combined.
  11. Pour the filling gently into the prepared smear crust, smoothing the top. Sprinkle generously with ground cinnamon.
  12. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes, or until the top is set and slightly golden.
  13. Serve warm from the oven or chilled from the fridge (many prefer chilled).

Notes

  • All eggs used in this recipe are Size 7 (large).
  • The smear crust recipe may give extra; leftover crust freezes well.
  • Store milk tart in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days.

Greek Yoghurt (EasiYo)

Economical, thick, and creamy Greek yoghurt using EasiYo base and extra milk powder. No straining, no thickeners, no preservatives.
Prep Time: 5 minutes (plus 24 hours incubation) | Cook Time: 0 minutes | Servings: Makes 1kg

Ingredients

  • Cold tap water
  • 180g full-cream milk powder
  • 3 tbsp greek yoghurt (or 3 tbsp of plain EasiYo Greek yoghurt sachet)

Instructions

  1. Gather all ingredients and boil the kettle.
  2. In the yoghurt making canister, fill to the halfway mark with cold water. Add the yoghurt starter.
  3. Add the milk powder.
  4. Place the lid on and shake vigorously until the milk powder is dissolved. Remove the lid and fill to the fill line with more water. Screw the lid on tightly and shake again.
  5. Fill the EasiYo yoghurt maker to the fill line with boiling water.

  • If you don't have an EasiYo, you can use a slow cooker: preheat on low for 30 minutes, turn off, fill with boiling water, place your sealed container with the mixture inside, and incubate as above.

Lemon Poppy Seed Muffins

One of Kimmie's party tricks. We often make this recipe.

Ingredients

Muffins

  • 1 1/3 cups flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup butter (4 tablespoons)
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla (or almond)
  • zest of 2 small lemons (or 1 large lemon, about 2 tablespoons)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 3/4 cup plain fat free Greek yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon poppyseeds

Icing (see note)

  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 50 grams cream cheese, softened
  • 3-4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1-2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C and grease a muffin pan or line with cupcake liners.

Notes

  • Note that we don't tend to make the icing for this recipe. We usually just do a glaze with icing sugar and lemon juice.
  • If you are going to make this icing, you should halve it, as it makes a lot.

Yorkshire Puddings

Classic British side, crisp and hollow, perfect for roast dinners.
Prep Time: 35 minutes (including resting) | Cook Time: 15-25 minutes | Servings: 12 standard muffin sized puddings

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 150g all-purpose flour
  • 175g whole milk
  • 25g water
  • 2g kosher salt (about 1/2 teaspoon)
  • 100ml beef drippings, lard, shortening, or vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Combine eggs, flour, milk, water, and salt in a medium bowl and whisk until a smooth batter is formed. Let batter rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. For best results, refrigerate batter overnight or up to 3 days. Remove from refrigerator while preheating oven.
  2. Adjust oven rack to centre position and preheat oven to 230°C. Divide drippings (or other fat) evenly between two 8-inch cast iron or oven-safe non-stick skillets, two 6-well popover tins, one 12-well standard muffin tin, or one 24-well mini muffin tin. Preheat in oven until fat is smoking hot, about 10 minutes.
  3. Transfer pans or tins to a heat-proof surface and divide batter evenly between every well (or between pans if using pans). Wells should be filled between 1/2 and 3/4 of the way (or 1/4 for pans). Immediately return to oven. Bake until puddings have quadrupled in volume, are deep brown, crisp, and sound hollow when tapped. Smaller ones take about 15 minutes, popover/skillet-sized about 25 minutes.
  4. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Resting the batter overnight improves results.
  • Can use beef drippings, lard, shortening, or vegetable oil.
  • For best rise, ensure fat is smoking hot before adding batter.
